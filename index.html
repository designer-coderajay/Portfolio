I reverse-engineer transformers. For my thesis I measured how well a model's confidence predicts whether its explanation is faithful. The answer: almost not at all. The correlation is r = 0.009, measured with causal circuit analysis on GPT-2 Small.

I founded Glassbox AI and turned the method into glassbox, an open-source toolkit that finds the circuit behind a prediction and writes EU AI Act Annex IV documentation from it, plus a preprint on arXiv. Before that: reproducible RAG and ML pipelines on Azure, agentic systems on LangGraph and MCP.

What I want to work on next: faithfulness metrics beyond ERASER, whether circuits found on toy tasks tell us anything about frontier models, and how far an interpretability audit can be pushed toward regulator-ready evidence.

The finding: this 6-head circuit explains 61.4% of the logit difference and is 100% sufficient on its own. Ablate it and backup heads keep about 78% of the signal flowing (1 minus comprehensiveness of 22%). An explanation scored on sufficiency alone looks more complete than it is. Head groups after Wang et al., 2022. Hover any head for its role, and press the button to ablate.

Findings

  1. Model confidence is not faithfulness.
    r = 0.009, confidence vs. explanation comprehensiveness · GPT-2 Small, IOI

    If you use a logit to decide whether to trust an explanation, you are reading noise. The two are causally disconnected, at least in this setting.

  2. 100% sufficient, only 22% comprehensive.
    6-head IOI circuit · 61.4% of logit difference · backup heads carry the rest

    Run alone, the circuit reproduces the full behavior. Remove it and backup heads pick up most of the slack. Score an explanation on sufficiency only and that redundancy stays invisible.

  3. Fluency inflates trust: 60% to 99%, with zero faithfulness gain.
    Perceived quality of LLM-written explanations (ERASER)

    Perceived quality jumped from 60% to 99% while the underlying evidence stayed the same. My pipeline scores the computation, not the prose.

Glassbox AI

pip install glassbox-mech-interp. The toolkit finds the causal circuit behind a transformer prediction with activation patching and scores it for sufficiency and comprehensiveness. One function call also writes the EU AI Act Annex IV documentation sections as structured JSON. It ships with a CLI, a test suite, and CI. When a model cannot actually do a task, it returns F1 near zero and refuses to certify it.
